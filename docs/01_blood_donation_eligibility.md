# Blood Donation Eligibility Criteria
**Australian Red Cross Lifeblood — Donor Eligibility Guidelines (Version 4.2)**

---

## 1. Overview

Australian Red Cross Lifeblood collects whole blood, plasma, and platelets from voluntary, unpaid donors across Australia. All donors must meet minimum health and safety criteria before each donation to protect both the donor and the recipient of blood products.

---

## 2. General Eligibility

### 2.1 Age
- Whole blood donors must be **aged 18–76 years**.
- First-time donors must be under 71 years of age.
- Donors aged 71–76 may continue donating if they have donated at least once in the preceding two years.

### 2.2 Weight
- Minimum body weight: **50 kg**.
- There is no upper weight limit, however donors must be able to comfortably use the donation chair.

### 2.3 General Health
- Donors must feel well on the day of donation.
- Donors must not be taking antibiotics for an active infection.
- Donors must not have had a fever (≥ 38 °C) in the past 7 days.

---

## 3. Deferral Periods

### 3.1 Travel-Based Deferrals
| Region Visited | Deferral Period |
|---|---|
| Malaria-endemic country | 4 months after return |
| Country with active Zika outbreak | 28 days after return |
| UK (1980–1996 for > 6 months cumulative) | Permanent deferral (vCJD risk) |

### 3.2 Procedure-Based Deferrals
| Procedure | Deferral Period |
|---|---|
| Tattoo or piercing (registered studio) | 4 months |
| Tattoo or piercing (unregistered) | 12 months |
| Endoscopy | 4 months |
| Major surgery | 12 months |
| Minor surgery / dental extraction | 7 days after healing |

### 3.3 Illness-Based Deferrals
| Condition | Deferral Period |
|---|---|
| Cold or flu (recovered) | 7 days after symptom resolution |
| COVID-19 (mild, recovered) | 7 days after symptom resolution |
| COVID-19 (hospitalised) | 4 months |
| Glandular fever | 12 months after full recovery |

---

## 4. Permanent Deferrals

Donors are permanently deferred if they:
- Have ever received a diagnosis of HIV, HTLV, or hepatitis B or C.
- Have ever injected recreational drugs (not prescribed).
- Lived in the UK for more than 6 cumulative months between 1980 and 1996.
- Have received a human pituitary extract (HGH or FSH) manufactured before 1986.
- Have received a corneal, scleral, or dural graft.

---

## 5. Iron & Haemoglobin Requirements

A fingerprick haemoglobin check is performed before each whole-blood donation.

| Donor Sex | Minimum Haemoglobin |
|---|---|
| Female | 125 g/L |
| Male | 135 g/L |

Donors with haemoglobin below threshold are deferred for **3 months** and advised to discuss iron supplementation with their GP.

---

## 6. Donation Frequency Limits

| Donation Type | Maximum Frequency |
|---|---|
| Whole blood | Every 12 weeks (up to 4× per year) |
| Plasma | Every 2 weeks (up to 26× per year) |
| Platelets | Every 2 weeks (up to 24× per year) |
