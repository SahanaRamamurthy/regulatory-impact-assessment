# Blood Safety and Testing Procedures
**Australian Red Cross Lifeblood — Quality & Safety Reference**

---

## 1. Introduction

Every blood donation collected by Australian Red Cross Lifeblood undergoes a mandatory battery of laboratory tests before any component can be released to hospitals. These tests are designed to detect infectious diseases that can be transmitted through blood transfusion and to verify the blood group of the donation. Australia has one of the safest blood supplies in the world, with the residual risk of transfusion-transmitted infection among the lowest globally.

---

## 2. Mandatory Infectious Disease Screening

All donations are tested for the following at Lifeblood's accredited testing laboratories:

### 2.1 Viral Markers

| Pathogen | Test Type | Detection Window |
|---|---|---|
| HIV-1 and HIV-2 | Antibody + NAT (PCR) | NAT window ~9 days |
| Hepatitis B virus (HBV) | HBsAg + Anti-HBc + HBV NAT | NAT window ~20 days |
| Hepatitis C virus (HCV) | Antibody + NAT | NAT window ~5 days |
| Human T-cell lymphotropic virus (HTLV I/II) | Antibody | — |
| West Nile Virus (WNV) | NAT (seasonal/risk-based) | — |

> **NAT** = Nucleic Acid Testing (PCR-based). NAT detects viral genetic material directly and significantly shortens the "window period" compared to antibody tests alone.

### 2.2 Bacterial Markers
| Pathogen | Test Type |
|---|---|
| *Treponema pallidum* (Syphilis) | Antibody (TPPA or equivalent) |
| Bacterial contamination (platelets) | Aerobic & anaerobic culture |

### 2.3 Parasitic Markers
| Pathogen | Test / Deferral |
|---|---|
| Malaria (*Plasmodium* spp.) | Risk-based deferral (4 months after travel to endemic region); no routine serological test on all donations |
| *Trypanosoma cruzi* (Chagas disease) | Antibody test for at-risk donors (born/lived in endemic Latin American countries) |

---

## 3. Blood Group and Phenotyping

Each donation is ABO and RhD typed. Additional extended antigen phenotyping is performed on donations from specific donor groups (e.g., Indigenous Australian donors) to improve matched blood availability for alloimmunised patients.

---

## 4. Residual Risk Estimates (Australia)

Due to NAT screening and rigorous donor selection, the estimated residual risk per unit transfused is extremely low:

| Pathogen | Estimated Residual Risk |
|---|---|
| HIV | 1 in 1,700,000 donations |
| HBV | 1 in 450,000 donations |
| HCV | 1 in 2,300,000 donations |

These figures represent the probability that a donation collected during the serological and NAT window period would pass all testing despite the donor being infected.

## 5. Pathogen Reduction Technology (PRT)

Lifeblood applies **pathogen reduction technology (PRT)** to plasma and platelet components:

### 5.1 INTERCEPT Blood System (Plasma & Platelets)
- Uses **amotosalen** (a psoralen compound) plus **UVA light** to cross-link nucleic acids in pathogens and residual leucocytes, rendering them unable to replicate.
- Effective against a broad range of viruses, bacteria, and parasites.
- Does **not** inactivate prions (vCJD risk is managed through donor deferral rather than PRT).

### 5.2 Methylene Blue Treatment (Plasma, select products)
- Used for single-unit fresh frozen plasma in some contexts.
- Methylene blue + visible light disrupts pathogen lipid envelopes and nucleic acids.

---

## 6. Lookback and Traceability

Lifeblood maintains comprehensive records linking each donation to its collected components and the hospitals/patients that received them. If a donor is later found to be infected with a transfusion-transmissible pathogen:

1. All previous donations are traced (lookback period up to 10 years for certain pathogens).
2. Hospitals are notified so that recipient patients can be counselled and tested.
3. Any remaining units from the same donor in inventory are recalled.

---

## 7. Donor Post-Donation Information (PDI) System

Donors who realise after donation that they may have been at risk of infection can contact Lifeblood to request that their donation not be used. This system — called **Post-Donation Information (PDI)** — allows the donation to be quarantined before it reaches a patient.

Contact: **13 14 95** (24 hours, 7 days a week for PDI reports)

---

## 8. Accreditation

Lifeblood's testing laboratories are accredited by the **National Association of Testing Authorities (NATA)** and comply with:
- TGA (Therapeutic Goods Administration) requirements for blood and blood components.
- ISO 15189 Medical Laboratory standards.
